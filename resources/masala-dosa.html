Masala Dosa



Recipe Source: Gemini AI

Masala dosa is a popular South Indian dish made from fermented rice and lentil batter,
filled with a spiced potato mixture.

Ingredients:

For the Dosa Batter:

  • 1/2 cup urad dal (whole black gram)
  • 1 cup parboiled rice (sona masuri or idli rice)
  • 1 tablespoon chana dal (split Bengal gram) (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • Salt to taste

For the Masala Filling:

  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 teaspoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped (adjust for spice preference)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/2 inch ginger, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust for spice preference)
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dosa Batter:
    2. Wash and rinse the urad dal, rice, and chana dal (if using).
    Soak them in separate bowls with enough water for at least 6-8 hours, or overnight.
    Grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds into a smooth batter. Add water for pouring consistency.
    Grind the soaked rice into a fine powder.
    Combine both batters, add salt, and mix well.
    Cover and let it ferment for at least 12 hours, or until doubled and slightly sour.

  2. Prepare the Masala Filling:
    3. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
    Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    Add green chilies and curry leaves, saute for a few seconds.
    Add chopped onions and saute until translucent.
    Add cumin seeds and ginger, saute for another minute.
    Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
    Add mashed potatoes and mix well.
    Cook for a few minutes, then garnish with coriander leaves (optional).

  3. Cook the Dosa:
    4. Heat a griddle or pan over medium-high heat.
    Reduce heat to medium-low and wipe the pan with a damp cloth.
    Pour a thin layer of batter, spreading it into a thin dosa.
    Drizzle oil around the edges.
    Once the edges brown and the bottom looks cooked, carefully flip the dosa.
    Cook for another 30 seconds to a minute, until golden brown.
    Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of the dosa.
    Fold the bottom and sides inwards to form a cone shape.
    Serve hot with sambar, coconut chutney, or any other desired chutney.
Tips:
    Thin the batter with water if needed.
    Use a non-stick pan for easier flipping.
    Cook longer on low heat for a crispier dosa.
    Adjust spice level to your preference.
    Experiment with different fillings.
Enjoy your delicious homemade masala dosa!
